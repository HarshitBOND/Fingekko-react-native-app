# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Primarily young adults building their first real money habits — comfortable with apps, new to budgeting discipline, and motivated more by momentum than by spreadsheets. They also use Fingekko to track shared expenses with friends or roommates and to hold savings goals, so a secondary context is anyone splitting bills within a friend group. They open the app in short, frequent sessions: checking a streak, logging an expense, seeing where a group settlement stands.

## Product Purpose

Fingekko is a gamified personal finance app that unifies solo budgeting, savings goals, and Splitwise-style group expense splitting in one place, using streaks, quests, and XP to turn the habit of tracking money into something people actually keep doing. Success is a user who both builds a consistent daily habit (opens the app, logs expenses, keeps a streak) and feels genuinely in control — clear on their spending, their goals progress, and who owes whom.

## Positioning

Unlike single-purpose finance apps, Fingekko is the one place for both your own money and the money you share with other people — personal budgeting, savings goals, and group expense splitting under one calm, game-like system that makes the habit stick.

## Brand Personality

Calm, premium, encouraging. It should never make money feel stressful or gamification feel juvenile — the tone sits closer to a trusted premium finance app that happens to reward consistency, not a rewards app that happens to track money. Encouragement comes through in copy and motion (celebrating a kept streak, a funded goal), not through loud, cartoonish UI.

## Anti-references

Not a garish, cartoon-mascot gamified rewards app (Duolingo-owl energy — badges and confetti as the point rather than a quiet accent). Not a spreadsheet-like legacy finance app (dense tables, no hierarchy, utilitarian gray). Positive references: Acorns, Emma, Monarch, Copilot — calm, premium fintech with soft rounded surfaces, layered elevation, and generous spacing.

## Design Principles

- Calm before clever: the gamification (streaks, quests, XP) is a layer of encouragement on top of a genuinely calm, premium finance UI — never the other way around.
- One system for solo and shared money: personal transactions, goals, and group splits should feel like one coherent product, not three bolted-together features.
- Momentum is the metric: every screen should make it obvious whether the user is on track, without requiring them to do math.
- Adaptive, not identical: the brand look stays constant across iOS and Android, but gestures, navigation, and system affordances should still feel native to each platform.
- Clarity over density: money numbers and shared-debt states must be scannable at a glance — no screen should require the user to cross-reference to understand where they stand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text and status colors (income/expense/savings) must hit AA contrast in light mode, status must never be conveyed by color alone (pair with icon/label), text should scale with system font-size settings, and motion (streak celebrations, transitions) needs a reduced-motion alternative.
